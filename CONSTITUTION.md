# THE LIQUIDGOV CONSTITUTION (v1.0.0)

## Article I: The Principle of Voluntaryism
LiquidGov is a distributed voluntary state. No citizen shall be coerced into participation, funding, or obedience without explicit, revocable consent. The power of the state is derived solely from the voluntary delegation of its members.

## Article II: Cognitive Meritocracy
Governance is not a popularity contest but a search for expertise. Through **Liquid Delegation**, citizens empower those they trust in specific subject domains. Reputation is earned through successful delivery of public value and verified by random citizen juries.

## Article III: The Economic Engine
Resource allocation follows the principle of **Quadratic Funding**. Small individual contributions are matched by a central treasury to amplify projects with broad community support, ensuring that the needs of the many outweigh the whims of the few.

## Article IV: Sybil Resistance & Privacy
The integrity of the state depends on the verification of unique human actors. **Zero-Knowledge Proofs** allow citizens to participate and vote with absolute privacy while maintaining cryptographic proof of their right to govern.

## Article V: Code as Law
Executable proposal data schemas ensure that once the community has spoken, execution is autonomous. Payouts are milestone-based and released only upon verification by the decentralized jury system.

## Article VI: The Executive Protocol
The maintenance of the state's digital infrastructure is governed by an autonomous protocol. This ensures that the platform remains synchronized, reconciled, and secure without the need for a permanent administrative class.

**LIQUIDGOV: GOVERNANCE AT THE SPEED OF THOUGHT.**
