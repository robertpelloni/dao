# VISION

## Ultimate Goal
The ultimate goal of the **dao** project (codenamed **LiquidGov**) is to create a distributed, voluntary government organization platform. It enables communities to form committees around specific subjects, propose actions, vote on them using innovative mechanisms like Quadratic Voting, and crowdfund infrastructure or public services via milestone-based assurance contracts.

## Design Philosophy
- **Decentralized & Voluntary:** Participation is choice-based and not coerced.
- **Expertise-Driven:** Leverages liquid delegation to allow experts to lead while maintaining democratic oversight.
- **Accountability:** Milestone-based funding ensures that resources are only released upon proven progress.
- **Sybil Resistance:** Uses unique human identity verification to ensure "one person, many voices" (via QV) rather than "one bot, one vote".
- **Structured Governance:** Proposals are not just text but executable data schemas.

## Core Pillars
1. **Identity Layer:** Sybil-resistant, privacy-preserving (ZKP) identity.
2. **Committee Layer:** Subject-specific, fluid networks with liquid delegation.
3. **Proposal Layer:** Structured metadata and milestone-based lifecycles.
4. **Quadratic Engine:** Quadratic Voting (QV) for preference intensity and Quadratic Funding (QF) for capital matching.
